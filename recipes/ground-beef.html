Ground Beef

How to Make Life-Changing Ground Beef

This recipe is simple and requires very few ingridients. Best results are found using a cast-iron skillet. You will bring your skillet to a medium-high heat on the stove.

Next, you will put the ground-beef into the skillet (1-lb recommended). Once your ground-beef has started to cook, season generously with salt, pepper, chili powder, and cayenne pepper. It is important to start breaking the ground beef up using a spatula at this time. Keep the pieces of ground beef moving. Now, you want to make sure that all of the water from within the beef has boiled out. The beef may look done at this point, but I can assure you it is not. Water boils at 212 degrees fahrenheit. You need more heat than that to capture all of the flavor. What we are looking for is called the Maillard Reaction. This is where the ground beef crispens and darkens a bit. This is where all the flavor is at. Once the majority of your ground beef has gotten a crispy, brown outside to it, you are all done.

Ingridients

  • 1-lb Ground Beef
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Chili Powder
  • Cayenne Pepper

Steps

  1. Bring your skillet to a medium-high heat on the stove
  2. Put the ground-beef into the skillet
  3. Once your ground-beef has started to cook, season generously with salt, pepper, chili powder, and cayenne pepper
  4. start breaking the ground beef up using a spatula
  5. Keep the pieces of ground beef moving by stirring and chopping into chunks
  6. Make sure that all of the water from within the beef has boiled out
  7. Wait for the ground beef to start to crispen and brown on the outside
  8. Once the majority of your ground beef has gotten a crispy, brown outside to it, remove the skillet from the heat
